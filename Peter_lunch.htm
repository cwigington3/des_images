Oisa Ramen

Oisa Ramen on Yelp

Chef Moe is cooking up delicious ramen like a boss in this small spot nestled between Bostonia and Tiki Rock. Unlike most other places, her main ramen stock is vegetable based which is unexpectedly packed with umami. For the omnivores out there she tops her ramen with delicious pork belly. This being a slurp and go, and a tiny restaurant, her ramen doesn't come out piping hot so you don't have to wait for it to cool and bcan quickly dig in. The ramen is eat in, but she does have to go plates including poke and other specials like chashu dogs. For the summer months, she recently debuted chilled ramen dishes which are very refreshing and delicious. Ask for it with extra spicy paste! It's packed during the lunch hours, but usually the wait isn't terrible and the line during dinner is pretty short. Chef Moe and her staff are also welcoming and are a wonderful addition to the neighborhood.

State Street Sushi

State Street Sushi on Yelp

Is this State Street's best kept secret? It just might be. The place is clearly meant for take out more than dine-in - which may be why the reviews are few and far between. Nonetheless, State Street Sushi is a no frills small joint serving up good quality sushi. You can place your specific order online or you can stroll in take a peek in the cooler/fridge where there are plenty of pre-made rolls (individual or in combos). The thing I love about State Street is that the fish is fresh but also they aren't loading up on rice. I love ordering Futo Maki rolls and they have been a true testament to how cheap and stingy a restaurant is being if they are loaded with rice versus loaded with filling (filling varies by restaurant but here it includes sweet egg, tobiko, cukes, avo, & crab). State Street serves up fat rolls that are light on rice! My type of place. The Winter Maki with salmon, yellowtail, scallions, and cucumber inside and salmon, avo, & tobiko on top is top notch. Be warned, if you get the Yellowtail Jalapeno roll, it comes with a mild/slightly pickled jalapeno instead of the a fresh jalapeno which you may come to expect. This is definitely going to be my go-to spot for sushi in FiDi now.


Only open for lunchtime, State Street Sushi has a couple of stools at their counter. It's clearly designed for takeout. When I popped in after lunch but before closing, they were still slicing fish and preparing. I grabbed a couple of boxes out of the cooler and took it to go. Miso soup included and it was tasty with a rich broth. Two sets of rolls were both fresh and well assembled. California roll with salmon and cream cheese and crunchy cucumber was great. Another with avocado and tuna and salmon roll plus a set of salmon and tuna nigiri was a great portion. Really good stuff!
